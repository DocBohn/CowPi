\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
Provides a font to display approximations of printable ASCII characters on 7-\/segment displays. 

\begin{DoxyAuthor}{Author}
Christopher A. Bohn
\end{DoxyAuthor}
The mapping of bits to segment lines is\+: 
\begin{DoxyCode}{0}
\DoxyCodeLine{ -\/-\/A-\/-\/}
\DoxyCodeLine{|     |}
\DoxyCodeLine{F     B}
\DoxyCodeLine{|     |              bit      7 6 5 4 3 2 1 0}
\DoxyCodeLine{ -\/-\/G-\/-\/           segment     DP A B C D E F G}
\DoxyCodeLine{|     |}
\DoxyCodeLine{E     C}
\DoxyCodeLine{|     |}
\DoxyCodeLine{ -\/-\/D-\/-\/   (DP)}

\end{DoxyCode}
 If your display follows the same mapping, you can use the functions\textquotesingle{} outputs directly. If the endianness of segments A-\/G are the same but your decimal point is bit 0, then you can left-\/shift the functions\textquotesingle{} outputs by 1 and only certain punctuation characters will be improperly displayed. If the mapping is any further off, then you will likely not be able to use these functions.
